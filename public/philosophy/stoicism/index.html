5 Core Principles

Focus on What You Control

The fundamental Stoic principle of distinguishing between what is within our power and what is not.

Key aspects:
  • Our thoughts and actions
  • Our responses to events
  • Our values and choices

Virtue as the Highest Good

Living according to the four cardinal virtues: wisdom, justice, courage, and temperance.

Key aspects:
  • Wisdom in decision-making
  • Justice in relationships
  • Courage in adversity

Acceptance of Fate

Embracing what happens to us while focusing our energy on our response and attitude.

Key aspects:
  • Amor fati (love of fate)
  • Present moment awareness
  • Emotional equilibrium

Negative Visualization

Imagining loss to increase gratitude and reduce attachment to external things.

Key aspects:
  • Appreciation of what we have
  • Preparation for setbacks
  • Reduced anxiety about loss

Evening Reflection

Daily practice of reviewing our actions, thoughts, and progress in virtue.

Key aspects:
  • Self-examination
  • Learning from mistakes
  • Continuous improvement
