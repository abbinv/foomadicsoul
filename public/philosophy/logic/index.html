5 Logical Principles

Deductive Reasoning

Drawing specific conclusions from general premises with logical certainty.

Key aspects:
  • General to specific
  • Logical certainty
  • Valid arguments

Inductive Reasoning

Drawing general conclusions from specific observations and evidence.

Key aspects:
  • Specific to general
  • Probability-based
  • Pattern recognition

Logical Fallacies

Common errors in reasoning that undermine the validity of arguments.

Key aspects:
  • Ad hominem attacks
  • False dichotomies
  • Circular reasoning

Syllogistic Logic

Formal reasoning using premises to reach logically valid conclusions.

Key aspects:
  • Major premise
  • Minor premise
  • Logical conclusion

Critical Thinking

Systematic evaluation of information and arguments to form sound judgments.

Key aspects:
  • Evidence evaluation
  • Bias recognition
  • Sound reasoning
