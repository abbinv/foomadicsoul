⚖️ Ethics

5 Ethical Frameworks

Utilitarianism

Actions are right when they produce the greatest good for the greatest number of people.

Key aspects:
  • Greatest happiness principle
  • Consequentialist approach
  • Collective well-being

Deontological Ethics

Moral duties and rules determine right action, regardless of consequences.

Key aspects:
  • Categorical imperatives
  • Universal moral laws
  • Duty-based morality

Virtue Ethics

Focus on character traits and what kind of person one should be.

Key aspects:
  • Character development
  • Moral virtues
  • Practical wisdom

Care Ethics

Emphasizes relationships, care, and responsibility in moral decision-making.

Key aspects:
  • Relational morality
  • Empathy and care
  • Contextual ethics

Applied Ethics

Practical application of moral principles to real-world issues and dilemmas.

Key aspects:
  • Bioethics
  • Business ethics
  • Environmental ethics
