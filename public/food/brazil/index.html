Feijoada Nacional

Brazil's national dish, feijoada, combines black beans with various pork cuts in a hearty stew traditionally served on Wednesdays and Saturdays. This dish originated from Portuguese and African influences, with enslaved Africans creating flavorful meals from available ingredients. Modern feijoada includes rice, collard greens, orange slices, and farofa (cassava flour mixture).
