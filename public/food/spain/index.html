Spanish cuisine reflects the country's diverse geography and rich history, from Moorish influences in Andalusia to Basque culinary innovations in the north. With 17 autonomous regions each offering distinct specialties, Spain's food culture emphasizes communal dining, quality ingredients, and time-honored traditions passed through generations.

Paella Mastery

Originating in Valencia's rice fields, authentic paella uses bomba rice, saffron, and regional ingredients like rabbit, chicken, beans, and snails. Seafood paella from coastal areas features prawns, mussels, and squid, while modern variations include vegetarian options. The socarrat (crispy bottom layer) indicates perfect preparation in traditional paelleras.
